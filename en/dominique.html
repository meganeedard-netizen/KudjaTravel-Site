The unspoilt island

Dominica

Overview

Dominica, nestled between Martinique and Guadeloupe, is one of the most unspoilt islands in the Caribbean. With waterfalls, rivers, hiking trails and secluded beaches, it will captivate any traveller in search of wild nature and a warm welcome from its people. A total change of scenery, guaranteed.

Must-see places in Dominica:

01 — Portsmouth

Portsmouth

Portsmouth, Dominica's second largest town, is a lovely destination with plenty to offer. Visit Fort Shirley in Cabrits National Park, enjoy the black sand beaches and take a boat trip along the Indian River.

02 — Morne Trois Pitons

Morne Trois Pitons

The Morne Trois Pitons National Park, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is a true gem of Dominica. The hike to the Boiling Lake is the most spectacular hike in the Caribbean!

03 — Scotts Head

Scotts Head

Scotts Head is a village at the southernmost tip of Dominica. This unique spot offers spectacular views over the Caribbean Sea and is also a very popular diving site.

04 — Soufrière

Soufrière

Soufrière is a charming fishing village in the south of Dominica, known for its hot springs and diving spots. At Champagne Reef, you can snorkel with volcanic bubbles rising from the seabed — a truly unique experience.

05 — Trafalgar

Trafalgar

Trafalgar is famous for its impressive twin waterfalls, the Trafalgar Falls. It is the perfect place to connect with nature and cool off in the heart of the Dominican rainforest.

06 — Batibou Bay

Batibou Bay

Batibou Bay is an idyllic beach on the north-east coast of Dominica. It is the perfect spot to disconnect and enjoy the island's natural beauty, far from any hustle and bustle.
